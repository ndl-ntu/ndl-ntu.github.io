## Research Directions

The Neuromorphic Device Lab studies device concepts that connect semiconductor materials, memory physics, sensing, and energy-efficient computing. Our work emphasizes experimentally grounded device characterization and application-aware neuromorphic functions.

<div class="research-theme">
  <img src="/assets/img/Retinomorphic.png" alt="Retinomorphic device concept">
  <div>
    <h3>Retinomorphic Vision Sensors</h3>
    <p>We develop bio-inspired optoelectronic devices and sensor concepts for dynamic machine vision, visual memory, and energy-efficient front-end processing.</p>
    <p><strong>Related work:</strong> Nano Research 2025; Advanced Intelligent Systems 2021; Advanced Intelligent Systems 2020.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="research-theme">
  <img src="/assets/img/EDL23.png" alt="Memory device characterization">
  <div>
    <h3>CBRAM and RRAM Devices</h3>
    <p>We investigate conductive-bridge and resistive switching devices for high-density memory, selectors, synaptic functions, and in-processor integration.</p>
    <p><strong>Related work:</strong> ACS Applied Electronic Materials 2024; Applied Physics Letters 2023; IEEE Electron Device Letters 2023.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="research-theme">
  <img src="/assets/img/fns.png" alt="Neuromorphic device illustration">
  <div>
    <h3>Neuromorphic Memory Devices</h3>
    <p>We study memory and transistor structures that emulate synaptic and neuronal behavior, including plasticity, leaky integration, and device-level learning functions.</p>
    <p><strong>Related work:</strong> Nanoscale Horizons 2023; IEEE Electron Device Letters 2022; ACS Applied Materials & Interfaces 2020.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="research-theme">
  <img src="/assets/img/NTU_EEE.png" alt="NTU EEE">
  <div>
    <h3>High-k Reliability And Trap Dynamics</h3>
    <p>We characterize charge trapping, gate dielectric reliability, hot-carrier effects, and nanoscale variability in advanced transistor and memory devices.</p>
    <p><strong>Related work:</strong> IEEE Transactions on Electron Devices; IEEE Electron Device Letters; Journal of Applied Physics.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Methods And Capabilities

* Electrical characterization of memory, selector, and transistor devices
* Reliability testing and trap dynamics analysis
* Device physics interpretation for neuromorphic and sensing functions
* Collaboration with industrial and academic partners on emerging semiconductor devices
